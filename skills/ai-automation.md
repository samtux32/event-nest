# AI & Automation

## AI Features Already Built

### AI Event Planner (Core Differentiator)
- **Technology**: Anthropic Claude SDK
- **Endpoint**: `POST /api/event-planner`
- **Component**: `components/AIEventPlanner.jsx`
- **What it does**: Customer inputs event details → Claude generates a full plan with budget breakdown, vendor category recommendations, timeline, checklist, and planning tips
- **Saved plans**: Plans persist in database (`SavedPlan` model) and can be edited, re-run, or linked to bookings
- **Marketing angle**: No competitor offers this. Lead with it in all marketing.

### AI-Generated Checklists
- Event planning checklists generated by AI based on event type and timeline
- Items include category, due date, and optional marketplace links
- Stored in database (`Checklist` + `ChecklistItem` models)

## AI for Marketing

### Content Generation
- Blog posts and inspiration articles: use Claude to generate first drafts targeting SEO keywords
- Social media captions: generate platform-specific copy for vendor spotlights and event content
- Email copy: draft marketing email sequences, subject lines, and CTAs
- Ad copy: generate Google and Meta ad variations for A/B testing
- Meta descriptions: generate SEO-optimised descriptions for programmatic pages

### Personalisation Opportunities
- **Vendor recommendations**: "Based on your wedding in June in Dublin, here are available photographers"
- **Wishlist-based suggestions**: "Vendors similar to ones you've saved"
- **Budget-aware recommendations**: AI planner already does this — extend to marketplace browsing
- **Seasonal prompts**: "Wedding season starts in 3 months — here's your planning checklist"

### AI for Vendor Support
- Profile optimisation suggestions: "Your bio is too short — here's a better version"
- Package pricing guidance: "Photographers in your area charge £800–1,500 — your price is competitive"
- Review response drafts: help vendors write professional responses to reviews
- FAQ generation: suggest FAQ entries based on common inquiries in the vendor's category

## Automation Workflows

### Customer Lifecycle (Build with Resend or Customer.io)

```
Registration
  ├→ Day 0: Welcome email + AI planner CTA
  ├→ Day 2: "Browse vendors in your area"
  ├→ Day 5: "Your first booking — here's how"
  └→ Day 10: "Planning tips for [event type]"

First Booking
  ├→ Instant: Booking confirmation
  ├→ Day 1: "Message your vendor with these details"
  └→ 3 days before event: Reminder notification (already automated via cron)

Post-Event
  ├→ Day 2: Review request (already automated via cron)
  ├→ Day 14: "Plan your next event"
  └→ Day 21: Referral prompt

Inactive (14+ days)
  ├→ Day 14: "New vendors in your area"
  └→ Day 30: "We miss you — here's what's new"
```

### Vendor Lifecycle

```
Registration
  ├→ Day 0: Welcome email + profile completion guide
  ├→ Day 2: "Add packages and portfolio"
  └→ Day 5: Profile completion reminder (if <80%)

Approval
  ├→ Instant: Approval email (already automated)
  └→ Day 3: "Tips from top vendors"

First Inquiry
  ├→ Instant: Push + email notification (already automated)
  └→ Day 1: "How to send a winning quote"

First Booking
  ├→ Instant: Confirmation notification (already automated)
  └→ Day 7: "Refer a vendor, earn credit"

Inactive (14+ days)
  ├→ Day 14: "Customers are searching for [category] in [city]"
  └→ Day 30: "Update your profile for [season]"
```

### Review Collection Automation (Already Built)
```
Booking completes (cron: 2 AM UTC)
  → Status updated to "completed"
  → Review request email sent to customer
  → If no review after 7 days → follow-up reminder (to build)
```

### Notification Automation (Already Built)
All significant actions trigger `createNotification()` which fires:
1. Database record (in-app)
2. Web Push via VAPID (fire-and-forget)
3. Email via Resend (fire-and-forget)

## Automation Principles for Event Nest

### Do Automate
- Post-event review collection (already done)
- Event reminders (already done)
- Booking status transitions (already done)
- Welcome and onboarding email sequences
- Vendor profile completion reminders
- Re-engagement campaigns for inactive users
- SEO meta tag generation for programmatic pages

### Don't Automate
- Vendor approval — keep human review for quality control
- Review moderation — flagged reviews need human judgement
- Custom quote creation — vendors should craft personalised quotes
- Complex customer support — route to human via contact form
- Pricing decisions — vendors set their own prices

### AI Safety Guardrails
- AI planner output should always include "This is a suggested plan — adjust based on your needs"
- Never auto-book vendors on behalf of customers — always require explicit confirmation
- AI-generated content (blog posts, emails) should be reviewed before publishing
- Don't use AI to generate fake reviews or vendor profiles
- Vendor data and customer conversations are private — don't use them for AI training
