# Partnerships & Community

## Partnership Types

### Venue Partnerships
Venues are the anchor of most events. A venue partnership means their clients discover Event Nest for all other vendor needs.

**How it works**:
- Venue lists on Event Nest as a vendor (Venue category)
- Venue recommends Event Nest to their clients for finding photographers, caterers, DJs, etc.
- Co-branded landing page: "Find Vendors for [Venue Name]"
- Venue displays Event Nest QR code at their location

**Value to venue**:
- Gets listed on a growing marketplace for free
- Provides a better service to their clients (one-stop vendor booking)
- Earns referral credit for vendors they bring onto the platform

**How to approach**:
- Target popular wedding and event venues in each city
- Lead with "We help your clients find the rest of their vendors — and it's free for you"
- Offer a branded page on Event Nest for their venue

### Vendor-to-Vendor Referrals
Vendors in complementary categories naturally refer each other. Event Nest can formalise and track this.

**Current system**:
- Referral codes and QR codes already built into the platform
- Vendors earn credit for referring other vendors who join

**Expansion ideas**:
- "Recommended by" badges: photographers recommend specific florists on their profile
- Vendor bundles: pre-packaged vendor combinations (photographer + videographer)
- Cross-category featured sections: "Vendors who work well together"

### Influencer & Creator Partnerships
Event content creators have audiences of people actively planning events.

**Target creators**:
- Wedding bloggers and vloggers
- Event styling/decor influencers on Instagram and TikTok
- Lifestyle creators who cover celebrations and parties
- Wedding planning YouTubers

**Partnership models**:
- Affiliate programme: creators earn commission on bookings driven through their link
- Sponsored content: creator features Event Nest in their planning content
- Exclusive access: creators get early access to new features or vendor showcases
- Content exchange: creators provide content for Event Nest's blog/social in exchange for exposure

### Industry Partnerships

**Event planning associations**:
- Get listed on industry directory sites
- Sponsor association newsletters
- Speak at or attend association events

**Wedding fairs and expos**:
- Sponsor or exhibit at local wedding fairs in target cities
- Distribute vendor signup materials and customer flyers
- Run QR code promotions: "Scan to browse all exhibitors on Event Nest"

**Complementary services**:
- Wedding dress shops (display Event Nest flyers for vendor booking)
- Party supply stores
- Event rental companies (tables, chairs, linens)
- Invitation and stationery designers

## Community Building

### Vendor Community
**Purpose**: Keep vendors engaged, sharing tips, and advocating for the platform.

**Channels**:
- WhatsApp group or Slack workspace for approved vendors
- Monthly vendor newsletter: platform updates, booking tips, seasonal trends
- Vendor spotlight programme: featured vendors on social media and blog

**Content for vendor community**:
- "How to write packages that convert"
- "Photography tips for your portfolio"
- "Seasonal pricing strategies"
- Success stories from top-performing vendors

### Customer Community
**Purpose**: Build word-of-mouth and repeat usage.

**Channels**:
- Instagram community: repost customer event photos (with permission)
- Email newsletter: seasonal planning tips, new vendor highlights, inspiration
- Inspiration articles on `/inspiration` (30 articles, growing to 100+)

**Engagement tactics**:
- "Share your event" campaign: customers post photos with #EventNest
- "Event of the month" feature on social media
- Planning tips from real customers who used Event Nest

## Local Market Entry Playbook

### Steps to Enter a New City
1. **Research**: Identify top 50 event vendors in the city (Instagram, Google, directories)
2. **Cold outreach**: Email/DM 50 vendors → aim for 10–20 signups
3. **Onboard**: Help vendors complete profiles, upload portfolios, set packages
4. **Approve**: Admin reviews and approves vendor profiles
5. **Seed reviews**: Encourage vendors to invite past clients to leave reviews
6. **Launch marketing**: Begin customer-facing marketing for that city
7. **Monitor**: Track vendor-to-customer ratio, inquiry rates, booking conversions
8. **Iterate**: Recruit more vendors in underserved categories based on demand data

### Cold Outreach Template (Vendors)
**Subject**: "Get bookings (not leads) — Event Nest for [City] vendors"

**Key messages**:
- We're building the event vendor marketplace for [City]
- Free to join, only pay 10% when you get a confirmed booking
- AI-powered platform sends you qualified inquiries
- Verified reviews help you stand out
- Join the first wave of vendors and get established early
