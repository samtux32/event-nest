# Strategy

## Growth Strategy

### Phase 1: Supply First (Current)
Build vendor supply in target cities before marketing to customers.
- Acquire 10–20 vendors per city across top categories (photography, catering, DJ, venue)
- Cold outreach to vendors via email and Instagram DM
- Vendor referral programme to grow organically
- Ensure profile quality: complete portfolios, packages with pricing, professional photos

### Phase 2: Demand Generation
Drive customer traffic to the marketplace once vendor supply is sufficient.
- SEO: programmatic pages for `/{category}-in-{city}` (e.g., "Wedding Photographers in Dublin")
- Content marketing: expand inspiration articles from 30 to 100+, targeting event planning keywords
- Social media: Instagram and TikTok vendor spotlights, event inspiration, behind-the-scenes content
- Google Ads: target high-intent searches ("book wedding photographer", "event caterers near me")

### Phase 3: Flywheel
Let network effects and reviews compound growth.
- Completed bookings → review requests → verified reviews → higher trust → more bookings
- Happy vendors refer other vendors → more supply → more customer choice
- Content ranks in search → organic traffic → marketplace browsing → bookings
- AI planner recommends vendors → bookings flow to well-profiled vendors → vendors invest in profiles

### Phase 4: Expansion
- Expand to new cities following the supply-first playbook
- Add new vendor categories based on demand signals
- Explore corporate event planning as a segment
- Consider international expansion to English-speaking markets

## Competitive Strategy

### vs Bark (lead-gen marketplace)
- **Our advantage**: Pay on results, not per lead. Vendors don't waste money on tyre-kickers.
- **Message**: "Stop paying for leads that go nowhere. Only pay when you win the booking."

### vs Poptop / AddToEvent (event marketplaces)
- **Our advantage**: AI event planner, modern UX, real-time messaging, better vendor tools.
- **Message**: "The modern event marketplace — with AI planning, instant messaging, and verified reviews."

### vs Bridebook / Hitched (wedding directories)
- **Our advantage**: Full marketplace with booking flow, not just a listing directory. Works for all events, not just weddings.
- **Message**: "More than a directory. Book, message, and manage vendors — all in one place."

### vs Instagram (DIY vendor discovery)
- **Our advantage**: Reviews, pricing, booking pipeline, calendar — everything Instagram doesn't have.
- **Message**: "Love their work on Instagram? Book them, message them, and manage everything on Event Nest."

## Channel Strategy

### Owned Channels (build these first)
- **Email list**: Customer and vendor lifecycle sequences. Event planning tips newsletter.
- **Blog/inspiration**: SEO-driven content that compounds over time.
- **Platform notifications**: Push and email keep users engaged.

### Rented Channels (use to funnel to owned)
- **Instagram/TikTok**: Vendor spotlights, event content. Drive to marketplace.
- **Pinterest**: Event inspiration boards linking to category pages.
- **Google Search**: Organic rankings for event planning keywords.
- **Facebook Groups**: Engage in local event planning communities.

### Borrowed Channels (amplify reach)
- **Wedding blogs and event publications**: PR and guest posts.
- **Vendor social accounts**: Vendors share their Event Nest profiles with their audiences.
- **Wedding fairs and event expos**: In-person presence in target markets.

## Key Strategic Decisions
- **Booking requires auth**: Customers must register to book — builds the user base, enables notifications and reviews
- **Vendor approval gate**: Admin must approve vendors before they appear in marketplace — ensures quality
- **Free for customers**: No paywall to browse, compare, or book — removes demand-side friction
- **AI as differentiator**: No competitor offers AI event planning — market it as the signature feature
- **City-by-city launch**: Don't market nationally until supply exists locally — prevents empty search results
