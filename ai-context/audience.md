# Target Audience

## Two-Sided Marketplace
Event Nest serves two distinct audiences. Both must be understood and marketed to separately — but the platform only works when both sides are healthy.

---

## Customers (Demand Side)

### Who They Are
- Individuals planning personal or corporate events
- Age range: 25–45, skewing female for weddings and birthdays, mixed for corporate
- Located in UK and Ireland (expanding)
- Range from first-time planners to people who organise events regularly

### Event Types They Plan
- Weddings (highest value, longest planning cycle)
- Birthday parties (most common, shorter planning cycle)
- Corporate events (team events, conferences, holiday parties)
- Baby showers and christenings
- Anniversaries and milestone celebrations
- Graduation and prom events
- Festivals and community events

### Jobs to Be Done
1. "Find a trustworthy vendor I can see reviews for before I commit"
2. "Get a clear price and compare options without awkward back-and-forth"
3. "Coordinate multiple vendors for one event without losing my mind"

### Pain Points
- **Scattered search**: Google, Instagram, Facebook groups, directories, friends — no single source of truth
- **No price transparency**: "Contact for a quote" means wasting time on vendors outside budget
- **Trust gap**: Can't verify vendor quality — Google reviews can be faked, Instagram only shows highlights
- **Coordination chaos**: 5+ vendors means 5+ separate message threads, no central view
- **High stakes**: A bad vendor choice ruins a once-in-a-lifetime day. The emotional cost is huge.

### How They Currently Search
- Google: "wedding photographer near me", "caterers in [city]"
- Instagram: browsing portfolios and hashtags
- Pinterest: event inspiration and mood boards
- Word-of-mouth: asking friends, family, colleagues
- Wedding fairs and bridal expos
- Facebook groups for local event recommendations

### What Converts Them
- Seeing real reviews with photos from actual events
- Transparent pricing (packages with clear costs)
- Being able to message vendors directly without commitment
- AI planner that makes the overwhelming feel manageable
- Free to use — no paywall to browse or book

## Vendors (Supply Side)

### Who They Are
- Small business owners and freelance professionals in event services
- Solo operators or teams of 2–10 people
- Mix of established businesses and newer entrants building their client base
- Located in UK and Ireland, serving local and regional markets

### Vendor Categories
- Photography (highest demand)
- Catering
- Videography
- Florist
- DJ
- Live Band / Musicians
- Venue
- Decorator / Stylist
- Cake

### Jobs to Be Done
1. "Get found by customers who are ready to book, not just browsing"
2. "Manage my bookings, calendar, and quotes in one place"
3. "Build a verified reputation that brings repeat business"

### Pain Points
- **Inconsistent lead flow**: Feast or famine — busy season is overwhelming, off-season is dead
- **Time wasted on tyre-kickers**: Responding to inquiries from people who ghost or were never serious
- **No central booking system**: DMs, email, phone, WhatsApp — error-prone and exhausting
- **Reputation building is slow**: Getting reviews requires manual follow-up, platforms don't help
- **Unfair platform fees**: Competitors charge per lead regardless of conversion — vendor loses money on bad leads

### Current Alternatives
- **Instagram**: Great for showcasing work, but no booking/messaging infrastructure
- **Own website**: No discovery — relies on SEO or paid ads the vendor manages themselves
- **Bark**: Broad lead-gen marketplace. Pay per lead, not per booking. Low conversion rates.
- **Poptop**: Similar marketplace but less modern, no AI features
- **Word-of-mouth**: Unpredictable, not scalable, hard to track

### What Converts Them
- "Only pay when you win the business" (10% on confirmed bookings, not per lead)
- Seeing the booking pipeline and quote tools — saves them admin time
- Analytics showing profile views and inquiry rates
- Verified reviews that build credibility they can point clients to
- Referral programme for growing through their network

---

## Marketplace Dynamics

### Supply-Demand Balance
- Must have vendor supply in a category and city BEFORE marketing to customers in that area
- Photography and catering are highest-demand categories — prioritise vendor acquisition there
- Wedding vendors drive the most revenue per booking — target them for vendor acquisition

### Network Effects
- More vendors → more choice for customers → more bookings → more vendors attracted
- More reviews → more trust → higher conversion rates → more bookings → more reviews
- AI planner recommendations drive bookings to vendors who complete their profiles fully

### Geographic Expansion Pattern
1. Seed a city with 10–20 vendors across top categories
2. Begin customer marketing in that city
3. Bookings and reviews create momentum
4. Vendor word-of-mouth attracts more vendors organically
