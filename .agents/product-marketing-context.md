# Product Marketing Context — EventNestGroup

## Product Overview
- **One-line**: Event Nest is a marketplace where customers discover, compare, and book verified event vendors.
- **What it does**: Connects people planning events (weddings, birthdays, corporate events, baby showers, anniversaries) with professional vendors (photographers, caterers, DJs, florists, decorators, videographers, venues, cake makers). Includes AI-powered event planning, direct messaging, custom quote negotiation, and a verified review system.
- **Product category**: Event vendor marketplace / event planning platform
- **Product type**: Two-sided marketplace (B2C on both sides)
- **Business model**: Free for customers. Vendors pay 10% fee on confirmed bookings. Customers pay 2% service fee. No subscription — vendors only pay when they get business.
- **Website**: https://eventnestgroup.com
- **Mobile**: iOS app via Capacitor (loads web app natively)

## Target Audience

### Customers (Demand Side)
- **Who**: Individuals planning personal or corporate events in the UK, Ireland, and beyond
- **Demographics**: 25–45, skewing female for weddings/birthdays, mixed for corporate
- **Primary use case**: Find and book reliable event vendors without the chaos of scattered Google searches, Instagram DMs, and word-of-mouth guesswork
- **Jobs to be done**:
  1. "Find a trustworthy vendor I can see reviews for before I commit"
  2. "Get a clear price and compare options without awkward back-and-forth"
  3. "Coordinate multiple vendors for one event without losing my mind"
- **Where they search**: Google ("wedding photographer near me"), Instagram (browsing portfolios), Pinterest (inspiration), word-of-mouth, wedding fairs
- **Budget awareness**: High — customers want transparent pricing upfront, not "contact for quote" black boxes

### Vendors (Supply Side)
- **Who**: Small business owners and freelance professionals in event services
- **Categories**: Photography, Videography, Catering, Florist, DJ, Live Band, Venue, Decorator/Stylist, Cake
- **Primary use case**: Get a steady stream of qualified booking inquiries without relying solely on Instagram or word-of-mouth
- **Jobs to be done**:
  1. "Get found by customers who are ready to book, not just browsing"
  2. "Manage my bookings, calendar, and quotes in one place"
  3. "Build a verified reputation that brings repeat business"
- **Current alternatives**: Instagram (no booking flow), own website (no discovery), Bark/Poptop (lead-gen model, pay per lead regardless of outcome), word-of-mouth (unpredictable)

## Problems & Pain Points

### Customer Pain Points
- **Scattered search**: Finding vendors means checking Google, Instagram, Facebook groups, wedding directories, and asking friends — no single source of truth
- **No price transparency**: Most vendors say "contact for a quote" — customers have no idea if they're in budget before reaching out
- **Trust gap**: Hard to verify vendor quality — reviews on Google can be faked, Instagram only shows the best work
- **Coordination chaos**: Planning a wedding with 5+ vendors means juggling separate email threads, WhatsApp groups, and spreadsheets
- **Emotional tension**: Event planning is stressful and high-stakes — a bad vendor choice ruins a once-in-a-lifetime day

### Vendor Pain Points
- **Inconsistent lead flow**: Feast or famine — busy season is overwhelming, off-season is dead
- **Time wasted on tyre-kickers**: Responding to inquiries from people who ghost or were never serious
- **No central booking system**: Managing bookings across DMs, email, and phone is error-prone
- **Reputation building is slow**: Getting reviews on Google or Facebook requires manual follow-up
- **Platform fees for leads, not results**: Competitors like Bark charge per lead regardless of conversion

## Competitive Landscape

### Direct Competitors
- **Bark** — lead generation marketplace (vendors pay per lead, not per booking). Broad services, not event-specific. Lower quality leads.
- **Poptop** — event vendor marketplace (UK). Similar model but less AI, less modern UX.
- **AddToEvent** — event entertainment marketplace. Narrower focus (DJs, bands, performers).
- **Bridebook / Hitched** — wedding-specific directories. Not full marketplaces (no booking/messaging).
- **Instagram** — where many vendors showcase work, but no booking infrastructure.

### Why Event Nest Wins
- **AI event planner**: No competitor offers AI-generated event plans with budget breakdowns and vendor recommendations
- **Pay on results, not leads**: Vendors only pay the 10% fee on confirmed bookings — no wasted spend on tyre-kickers
- **Full workflow**: Discovery → messaging → quotes → booking → reviews, all in one platform
- **Verified reviews with photos**: Not anonymous ratings — real customers, real event photos
- **Modern UX**: Built on Next.js with real-time messaging, push notifications, and mobile-first design
- **Free for customers**: No paywall to browse, compare, or book

## Key Features (for Marketing)

### Customer-Facing
1. **AI Event Planner** — input your event details, get a full plan with budget, timeline, and vendor recommendations
2. **Vendor Marketplace** — browse by category, location, price, and rating with real reviews and portfolios
3. **Direct Messaging** — chat with vendors, negotiate quotes, share files — no middleman
4. **Custom Quotes** — request and compare itemised quotes from multiple vendors
5. **Wishlists** — save and organise vendors into named groups for easy comparison
6. **Event Checklists** — AI-generated task lists with timelines to keep you on track
7. **Calendar Integration** — export bookings to your calendar with one click
8. **Push Notifications** — real-time updates on messages, quotes, and booking status

### Vendor-Facing
1. **Professional Profile** — portfolio, packages, pricing, FAQs, awards, social links
2. **Booking Pipeline** — manage inquiries from first contact to completion
3. **Quote Builder** — create detailed quotes with features and pricing
4. **Availability Calendar** — block dates, sync events
5. **Analytics Dashboard** — track profile views, inquiry rates, and booking conversions
6. **Verified Reviews** — build reputation with customer reviews and photos
7. **Referral Programme** — earn credit by referring other vendors
8. **QR Code** — share your profile at events and expos

## Metrics That Matter
- **Vendor-to-customer ratio** by category and city (marketplace health)
- **Booking conversion rate** — inquiry to confirmed booking
- **Vendor response time** — speed of first reply to inquiry
- **Review rate** — % of completed bookings that generate a review
- **AI planner usage** — % of customers who use the planner before browsing

## Geographic Focus
- **Primary**: UK and Ireland
- **Expansion**: English-speaking markets, then broader Europe
